Description:

Morse code serves as a technique for conveying textual information through a sequence of alternating tones, lights, or clicks, comprehensible to a proficient listener or observer without the need for specialized equipment. This communication method involves a combination of dots and dashes and is employed to transmit messages across extended distances. Initially utilized by telegraph operators who transmitted messages through electrical pulses, Morse code found subsequent adaptation by radio communicators, who transformed it into an auditory language.

Morse code is a versatile method for sending messages through wires, radios, sound, and light using tapping, drumming, buzzing, clicking, or flashing light. Widely used by ham radio operators today, it is recognized in the amateur radio community for its efficiency in long-distance communication. Morse code is also essential in emergency signals and technology, aiding communication for people with disabilities.
